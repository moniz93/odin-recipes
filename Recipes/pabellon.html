Authentic Venezuelan Pabellón Criollo Recipe

Embark on a culinary journey with our authentic Venezuelan Pabellón Criollo, a dish that captures the essence of Venezuela's rich gastronomic heritage. This classic recipe brings together tender shredded beef, black beans, rice, and sweet plantains in a harmonious medley of flavors and textures. The succulent shredded beef is slow-cooked to perfection, infused with a blend of spices that evoke the warmth of Latin American kitchens. Nestled beside it, the black beans simmered to a velvety consistency, offering a savory counterpart to the sweet and caramelized notes of the ripe plantains. The ensemble is completed with a mound of fluffy white rice, creating a plate that not only delights the palate but also tells a story of tradition and cultural richness. Elevate your dining experience with the vibrant and heartwarming flavors of our Authentic Venezuelan Pabellón Criollo – a celebration on every plate.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) beef flank or skirt steak
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups black beans (canned or cooked from dried)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 ripe plantains, peeled and sliced diagonally
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • Avocado slices

Steps:

  1. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef and pork over medium heat. Add chopped onions and garlic, cooking until softened.
  2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, red wine (if using), basil, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese with one beaten egg.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  6. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a portion of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Repeat the layers, finishing with a final layer of meat sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  8. Pour béchamel sauce evenly over the top layer.
  9. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  10. Allow the lasagna to rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.

Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!
